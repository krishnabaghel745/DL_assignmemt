Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted organization. This type of fraud is very common and usually happens through emails, messages, or fake websites.

In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives an email or message that appears to be from a legitimate source, such as a bank or online service. The message often creates a sense of urgency, asking the user to click on a link to verify their account or update information. Once the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks real. The victim unknowingly enters their login credentials, which are then captured by the attacker.

Students and general internet users are common targets because they may not always recognize fake messages. The consequences of phishing can be serious, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting online. Users should always verify the source of messages, avoid clicking on suspicious links, and use security measures such as strong passwords and two-factor authentication to protect themselves.
